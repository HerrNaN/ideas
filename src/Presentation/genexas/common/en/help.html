Symbols

We use simple ASCII signs to represent the logical symbols. The symbols are represented as follows:

ASCII characters to represent Logical symbols
Logical symbol ASCII character
Logical And∧ /\ (slash followed by backslash)

Implication→ -> (the small - followed by the greater than sign)
Equivalence↔ <-> (lesser than, small - , greater than)

Exercise Area

The Exercise Area in the upper left corner will hold the exercise on which you are currently working. You can't change its contents.

You will receive a new exercise when you click the New Exercise button that you see above the Feedback Area.

Work Area

Below the Exercise Area, you see the Work Area. This is the area which you use to rewrite the expression that you are trying to solve.

When you have asked for a new exercise, the expression will not only be visible in the Exercise Area, but also in the Work Area. The difference is that you can edit the Work Area.

When you think you have rewritten the expression into an equivalent expression toward a solution, you click the Submit button.

Buttons

Below the work area there are several buttons. Some of them are not always visible: buttons only appear when they are relevant.

From left to right and from top to bottom you may see:

  • Hint Any time, you may ask for a hint. The Exercise Assistent will propose a rule that is applicable on the current expression in the Work area.
    The name of the rule will appear in the Feedback area.
  • Step When a hint is not enough, you may ask for a step. The Exercise Assistent will show you not only the name of a rule that is applicable, but also the resulting expression.
    The name of the rule and the resulting expression will appear in the Feedback area, and the resulting expression will also be available under the Cop button (see below).
  • Derivation One step further is to ask for a derivation. The Exercise assistant will show you a complete solution, in single steps, with the name of the rule which is applied in each step, and the intermediate expression.
    The derivation will appear in the Feedback area.
  • Submit When you have rewritten the expression in the Work area, you can hit submit to check the result. The feedback will appear in the Feedback area.
    When the Exercise Assistant detects a mistake, it will try to explain you exactly where and how the mistake was made. The previous expression will be available under the Copy button, in case of a mistake.
  • Copy The Copy button is the first button of the second row.
    It will be available when it is relevant. It will paste the expression which is mentioned in the Feedback area to the Work area.
  • Back The Back button allows you to step back in time. The back button of the browser does not recognize the single steps you did take during the exercise; the Back button of the Exercise Assistent does.
  • Forward The same applies for the Forward button, which will appear after you have used the Back button.
  • Ready With the Ready button, you may check whether you have solved the exercise.

History

Below the Work area is the History area. This area will contain all valid steps of a single exercise

Feedback

The green area to the right is the Feedback area. All feedback will appear there.

The feedback will appear below the previous feedback. If you feel that the Feedback area becomes too cluttered, you may clear it with the Clear button above it.

Menu

The menu is formed by four buttons just below the title bar. From left to right you see:

  • New Exercise will clear all areas, and show a new exercise in the Exercise area.
  • Rewriting Rules will show you a list of the rewriting rules.
  • You used Help to see this text. The Help area will disappear when you use the Close button.
  • About will tell you some information about the Exercise Assistant.
